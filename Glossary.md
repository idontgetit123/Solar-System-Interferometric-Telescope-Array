# Glossary.md
## Solar System Baseline Interferometer (SSBI)
### Glossary of Terms

---

## ⭐ Overview
This glossary defines key scientific and engineering terms used throughout the SSBI documentation. It is designed to help readers of all backgrounds understand the concepts behind a **solar‑system‑scale interferometric imaging system**.

---

## ⭐ A

### **Aperture Synthesis**
A technique that combines signals from multiple telescopes to simulate a much larger telescope. SSBI uses AU‑scale baselines to achieve extreme resolution.

### **Atomic Clock**
An ultra‑precise timekeeping device used to maintain phase coherence across satellites separated by millions of kilometers.

### **AU (Astronomical Unit)**
The average distance between Earth and the Sun (~150 million km). SSBI uses baselines of 1 AU and beyond.

---

## ⭐ B

### **Baseline**
The distance between two collector satellites. Longer baselines = higher resolution.

### **Baseline Geometry**
The arrangement of satellites in different orbits to fill the Fourier plane for image reconstruction.

---

## ⭐ C

### **Collector Satellite**
A satellite equipped with optical/IR detectors, atomic clocks, and laser communication systems. Acts as a node in the interferometric array.

### **Cosmic Node**
A star system containing its own SSBI array, capable of synchronizing with other star‑system arrays across light‑years.

### **Clock Drift**
The gradual deviation of a clock’s timing. SSBI corrects this using predictive models and laser time transfer.

---

## ⭐ D

### **Data Pipeline**
The full process from photon capture → preprocessing → transmission → synthesis → final image.

### **Deep‑Space Laser Link**
A high‑bandwidth optical communication system used for timing and data transfer between satellites.

---

## ⭐ E

### **Exoplanet Atlas**
A long‑term archive of surface maps, climate cycles, and atmospheric data for nearby exoplanets imaged by SSBI.

### **Extrasolar Cartography**
The science of mapping planets outside our solar system.

---

## ⭐ F

### **Fourier Plane**
A mathematical space representing spatial frequencies. SSBI fills this plane using multi‑angle baselines to reconstruct images.

### **Fourier Transform**
A mathematical operation that converts visibility data into spatial frequency information.

---

## ⭐ G

### **GPU Cluster**
A group of graphics processing units used to perform high‑speed aperture synthesis and image reconstruction.

---

## ⭐ I

### **Interferometry**
A technique that combines light from multiple telescopes to measure interference patterns, enabling extremely high resolution.

### **Interstellar Node**
A future SSBI array located in another star system (e.g., Proxima Centauri), synchronized with the Solar System node.

---

## ⭐ L

### **Laser Time Transfer**
A method of synchronizing clocks across vast distances using optical signals.

---

## ⭐ M

### **Multi‑Wavelength Imaging**
Capturing data across visible, near‑infrared, and mid‑infrared bands to reveal different planetary features.

---

## ⭐ N

### **Node Network**
A distributed set of SSBI arrays across multiple star systems, forming a galaxy‑scale imaging instrument.

---

## ⭐ P

### **Phase Coherence**
The alignment of photon arrival times across satellites. Essential for interferometry.

### **Phase Closure**
A technique used to correct phase errors in interferometric data.

---

## ⭐ R

### **Relativistic Drift Correction**
Adjusting timing signals to account for relativistic effects on clocks in different gravitational or orbital environments.

---

## ⭐ S

### **Station‑Keeping**
Autonomous thruster adjustments that keep satellites in precise orbital positions.

### **Surface Resolution**
The smallest surface feature SSBI can distinguish on an exoplanet.

---

## ⭐ T

### **Time‑Tagged Photon Stream**
A dataset containing photon arrival times with picosecond precision.

### **Two‑Star Interferometry**
A future capability where SSBI arrays in two star systems combine data to create multi‑light‑year baselines.

---

## ⭐ V

### **Visibility Function**
A measurement of how light waves interfere across baselines. Used in Fourier synthesis.
