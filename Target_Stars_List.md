# Target_Stars_List.md
## Solar System Baseline Interferometer (SSBI)
### Target Stars List (20–50 Light‑Years)

---

## ⭐ Overview
This document lists nearby stars within **20–50 light‑years** that are strong candidates for SSBI imaging. These stars are bright enough, close enough, and astrophysically interesting enough to support **continent‑level exoplanet surface imaging** using AU‑scale baselines.

This list includes:
- Sun‑like stars  
- Known exoplanet hosts  
- High‑value targets for habitability studies  
- Multi‑star systems with stable habitable zones  

---

## ⭐ High‑Priority Targets (Closest + Most Scientifically Valuable)

### **Alpha Centauri System (4.3 ly)**
- Alpha Centauri A  
- Alpha Centauri B  
- Proxima Centauri  

### **Barnard’s Star (5.96 ly)**  
- High proper motion, potential planets

### **Wolf 359 (7.8 ly)**  
- Red dwarf, potential for close‑orbit planets

### **Lalande 21185 (8.3 ly)**  
- Bright M‑dwarf, nearby, stable

### **Sirius System (8.6 ly)**
- Sirius A  
- Sirius B  

### **Epsilon Eridani (10.5 ly)**  
- Known debris disk, possible planets

### **Procyon System (11.4 ly)**
- Procyon A  
- Procyon B  

### **61 Cygni A/B (11.4 ly)**  
- Twin K‑dwarfs, stable, long‑term targets

### **Tau Ceti (11.9 ly)**  
- Multi‑planet system, Sun‑like star

## ⭐ Purpose of This List
These stars represent the **first generation** of targets for SSBI’s exoplanet imaging program. As the system expands — and as additional SSBI nodes are established in other star systems — the target list will grow to include hundreds or thousands of stars across the local bubble.
