## Solar System Baseline Interferometer (SSBI)
### Mission Overview

---

## ⭐ Mission Summary
The Solar System Baseline Interferometer (SSBI) is a next‑generation astronomical imaging system that uses **AU‑scale baselines** to achieve unprecedented angular resolution. By distributing optical/IR collector satellites across solar orbits and combining their signals through interferometric synthesis, SSBI enables **direct imaging of exoplanet surfaces** within 20–50 light‑years.

This mission transforms the solar system into a **single, unified telescope**.

---

## ⭐ Mission Objectives
- Capture **continent‑level detail** on nearby Earth‑like exoplanets  
- Map **oceans, landmasses, ice caps, and cloud systems**  
- Monitor **seasonal and atmospheric changes**  
- Produce the first **Exoplanet Atlas**  
- Demonstrate solar‑system‑scale interferometry  
- Establish a long‑term, modular architecture for future expansion  

---

## ⭐ Mission Architecture
SSBI consists of:

- **Distributed satellite collectors** positioned across 1 AU baselines  
- **Laser‑linked timing systems** for phase coherence  
- **Autonomous orbital station‑keeping**  
- **Deep‑space GPU clusters** for aperture synthesis  
- **Multi‑wavelength imaging** (visible, NIR, MIR)  
- **Redundant orbital rings** for multi‑angle sampling  

The system is designed to scale from dozens to hundreds of satellites.

---

## ⭐ Scientific Impact
SSBI enables:

- Direct imaging of exoplanet surfaces  
- Climate and weather pattern analysis  
- Atmospheric composition studies  
- Rotation and axial tilt measurements  
- Long‑term habitability assessments  

This mission represents the first step toward **true extrasolar cartography**.

---

## ⭐ Mission Philosophy
SSBI is built on a simple principle:

**“We don’t just detect other Earths — we see them.”**

The mission is designed to be modular, expandable, and compatible with future technologies, allowing humanity to continuously improve its ability to observe distant worlds.
